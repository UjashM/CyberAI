AISCI Process

UAlbany researchers and scholars are invited to form multi-investigator teams to develop research whitepapers on a topic that spans AI and CS regimes. The ultimate goal of each team must be the development and submission of at least one grant application to a federal agency. To assist in the advancement of teams and identification of resources, prospective applicants may wish to visit the UAlbany Cybersecurity and Artificial Intelligence Resource managed by the Division for Research.

  1. The team must have at least three PIs from two or more UAlbany units (departments, schools, or colleges). The Division for Research strongly encourages inclusion of:
    1. Scholar(s) from social sciences, humanities, and the arts on the project team,
    2. Investigators from underrepresented groups and women, and
    3. Junior researchers, e.g., assistant professors or postdoctoral level scholars.
    4. Industry partners
  2. Priority for seed funding will be given to the projects that address problems implementing AI in CS. Projects that implement Quantum Information Science into their projects and/or propose funding that will foster their involvement in a Quantum LEAP Challenge Institute application will be seriously considered.
  3. The team must identify at least one federal agency and the funding program(s) that supports proposed research activities, submission deadline, and a timeline leading to proposal submission.
  4. The extramural proposal must request at least $500,000.
  5. The white papers will be reviewed by subject matter faculty experts and representatives of the School/College Deans and the Divisions for Research and Academic Affairs.
  6. The white papers selected for funding may be posted in non-disclosing representation, on the Division for Research website. All funded teams will be called upon to present the project and its expected outcomes to the campus community during UAlbany’s annual recognition events in 2020 or at other appropriate forums.
